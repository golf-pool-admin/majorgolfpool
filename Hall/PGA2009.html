2009 PGA Championship Golf Pool
Chaska, Minnesota
August 13-16, 2009

After several weeks of intense therapy, I am on the path to recovery from this year's Open Championship at Turnberry. The enormous physical and psychological damage inflicted by Mr. Tom Watson on your truly as I agonized over his play at  the stunning seaside Scottish landscape did take it's toll; however, as part of my rehabilitation, I've been advised by several therapists/pool aficionados to focus my attentions on this year's final major solely in the role of golf pool administrator. In the interests of my health, please join me in wagering on this year's final major! Fore!
Congratulations to Y.E. Yang on his first major victory!

Congrats to Sean Shannon and Team Orange for his pool win!
Y.E. Yang 2009 PGA Champ

Latest Pool News:  August 16, 2009 9:30 PM – Congratulations to Y.E. Yang who stood up to Tiger and surged to victory with a brilliant approach shot on 18. Y.E. kept his composure and lunch down, unlike Paddy who threw up all over himself. Tiger uncharacteristically did not make the putts, and shows us he is indeed human. Some great golf this afternoon.

To the pool: and it was the tighest race in pool history! Three teams tied at 2 over par for first place: Team Orange, Hole Lotta Love, and High Fives at the 12th. We had to go to the winning score pick and, again, it was one stroke separating all three players. Congratulations to Sean Shannon, Team Orange, who predicted the winning score bang on! Following in second is pool veteran Ian McLean’s Hole Lotta Love (pick -7), and Kim McKenny’s High Fives at the 12th  (pick -6).

I’ll double-check in the morning, but all looks well. Thanks for another great pool, folks. Full field scores and your top 8 are here.
